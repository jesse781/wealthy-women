The Research

Structural Inhibitors Facing Female Founders

Based on co-creative workshops conducted with Parsons ELab and the Diversified Capital Coalition, identifying the systemic barriers that limit access to capital.

Structural Barriers & Biases

Female founders face inequities at every step compared to men. Patriarchy, sexism, and racism negatively compound a woman's entrepreneurial experience.

Network Exclusion

Limited access to influential networks prevents women from connecting with key investors, mentors, and critical resources, hindering business growth.

Health Consequences

Women face operational challenges leading to long-term stress and negative health effects. They often undersell achievements and shy away from highlighting strengths.

False Perceptions

The "Holmes Effect" creates bias that women are less trustworthy, despite data showing female founders outperforming men.

Time Poverty Bias

Programs designed for those with ample time and money don't consider that women juggle dual responsibilities including family care and primary job duties.

Intergenerational Trauma

Systemic racism, patriarchy, and societal expectations are embedded in intergenerational transfer of trauma, perpetuating underrepresentation.

2.2% VC funding to female founders

Despite starting businesses at higher rates, women receive disproportionately less capital

Female founders in the U.S. continue to receive a disproportionately small share of venture capital—just 2.2% in 2024. While this represents a slight improvement from 1.8% in 2023, it still demonstrates a systemic failure that requires coordinated intervention.

Understanding the Barriers

Our research identified specific challenges across the funding ecosystem.

Structural Barriers & Biases

Patriarchy, sexism, and racism negatively compound at every step of a woman's entrepreneurial journey.

Motherhood Penalty

Women experience an average 4-6% loss in earnings while men increase earnings by an average of 4%.

Network Exclusion

Limited access to influential networks prevents connections with key investors, mentors, and resources.

Mental & Physical Health Impact

Operational challenges lead to long-term stress and its negative health effects throughout the funding journey.

Network Impact

Program managers rely heavily on personal networks rather than open market calls or conscious outreach to diverse communities.

Program Design Lacks Flexibility

Programs developed for those with ample time and money don't consider different founder lifestyles and needs—"time poverty bias."

Vetting & Selection Bias

Programs connected to VC use fund criteria that excludes founders in sectors other than scalable tech, which predominantly attracts men.

Lack of Safe Spaces

Women and POC are underrepresented, and events hosted in venues like whiskey or steak houses reinforce exclusion.

False Perceptions

The "Holmes Effect" creates bias despite data showing female founders outperforming men.

Risk Appetite & Herd Mentality

Investors mitigate volatility through self-limiting actions and herd mentality rather than evidence-based evaluation.

Traditions & Lack of Innovation

Investors lack imagination and willingness to diversify portfolios by sectors—traditional and conservative approaches persist.

Cascade Effect

Pension fund managers' restrictions set into motion "go with what you know" and "trusted and tested" mindsets throughout the chain.

Accountability Metrics

Early education and mentorship programs connecting young girls with successful female entrepreneurs through AI-powered matching and business simulations.

Smart Matching & Business Scenarios
